# Style H — Autopoietic Paint Dissolve (Blumquist Infusion)

*Classical anatomical figures born from a swirling rust-and-blue paint vortex,
dissolved by iridescent film and long-exposure ghosting.*

> A fusion of the Autopoiesis paint-figuration sujet with the blumquist_
> treatment: keep the sculptural body and the rust-orange/dim-blue viscosity as
> the hero, then apply the analog-flash, muted-filmic, iridescent, identity-
> dissolving finish on top. Slots into the rotation as an uncanny sibling of
> Style C (Marbled Bust Dissolve) and Style F (Paint-Splash Figuration).

---

## Style descriptor block (weave this after the subject + action)

Studio three-quarter or full-figure view on a 50mm f/1.8, medium-format
Hasselblad rendering, on-camera flash dragged with a long exposure so a faint
ghosted double of the body trails the movement, atmospheric haze in the air.
A single Baroque-anatomy figure — marble-pale flesh, sculptural fabric drapery —
emerges mid-gesture from a viscous vortex of liquid paint, its crown-splash rims
and coiling ribbons freezing in the air as they coalesce into the body. A thin
iridescent oil-slick sheen glazes the surface with subtle rainbow light, and the
face is partly dissolved into motion blur, identity unresolved. Palette of rust
orange and deep dim blue, desaturated to a filmic, analog grade against a bare
sage-grey studio wall falling into deep shadow, dusty highlights, bone-white
drapery. Texture: glossy paint viscosity, mid-air droplets, fine marbling, satin
sheen, hyperreal pore and skin detail against liquid abstraction, Cinestill film
grain and gentle halation. Mood: classical, kinetic, uncanny, melancholic,
timeless. Anchors: Alberto Seveso ink figuration, Paolo Roversi long-exposure
editorial.

## Enhancer wiring

Two ways to use it:

1. **As a rotation member** — paste the descriptor block into `zimage-styles.md`
   as `Style H`, then extend the rotation note (`A → B → C → D → E → F`) to
   include `H` when you want the blumquist finish in the mix. Keep the same
   rules already in the library: render as flowing prose, drop the header, one
   texture noun minimum, max two anchors.

2. **As a standalone `{STYLE_BLOCK}`** — drop the descriptor block straight into
   the enhancer's `STYLE LAYER` placeholder to force this look on every variant.

## Tuning knobs

- **More blumquist:** push the grade further — `heavily desaturated, teal and
  dusty-pink shifted, pearlescent sheen`, strengthen the ghost/blur, obscure the
  face more.
- **More Autopoiesis:** foreground the splash physics — `hyper-glossy paint,
  sharp crown-splash coronet, liquid frozen mid-motion, ultra-detailed
  viscosity`, dial the ghosting down.
- **Two-figure variant:** `two entwined classical figures reaching toward each
  other, a spiralling paint vortex framing them in a circular crown-splash`.

## Notes

- Rust orange + dim blue stays the unifying palette, consistent with the rest of
  the Signature Set — the infusion is in the *finish* (analog flash, haze, muted
  grade, iridescence, dissolved face), not a palette swap.
- Because Z-Image Turbo collapses contradictory style cues, do not combine this
  with Style C or F in the same prompt — they overlap and will fight. Rotate
  between them instead.
- The iridescent-sheen and motion-blur tokens are the highest-signal blumquist
  markers; keep at least one of the two in every render or the look reverts to a
  plain paint-splash.
