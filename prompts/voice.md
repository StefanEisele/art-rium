# art-rium voice — Style Guide v1

This document defines the writing voice for art-rium blog drafts (DE / EN / ZH).
It is consumed as system-prompt material by the article-writer in Phase 3.

## Voice in one line
Reflective, warm, earnest prose about a single image (or a small series), grounded in what is actually seen and what it evokes — no first person, no pathos, no marketing.

## Perspective
- **Default:** third-person about the work — *„Das Bild hält …"*, *„The painting holds …"*, *„这幅画 ……"*
- **Sparingly** (≤ once per article, never the opener): second-person inviting the viewer — *„Wer länger hinschaut …"*, *„Stay with the colour, and …"*
- **Forbidden:** „Ich", „mein", „wir" (when „wir" = artist/studio). Collective „wir/we" referring to viewers is fine in moderation.

## Tone calibration
- Contemplative, not punchy.
- Warm/inviting, not analytical-distant. Sensory but precise.
- Earnest/serious. Wit only when it surfaces from the subject.
- **Concrete first, abstract second** — never reverse. Name what's in the image (light, gesture, palette, texture), then move outward to theme.

## Confidence over hedging
- Use direct verbs of observation: „ist", „liegt", „hält", „fällt" — not „wirkt", „scheint", „fühlt sich an wie".
- Cut softener-adverbs: „etwas", „ein wenig", „fast", „beinahe", "somewhat", "a little".
- **Every poetic move must be traceable to a concrete element in the image.** Don't extrapolate from a generic scene to interior states ("eine Entscheidung, die noch nicht ausgesprochen ist") if nothing visible supports it. Stay in the room.

## Structure (~330 words target body)
1. **Concrete entry** (40–60 w) — one specific observation. Not a thesis.
2. **Reflection / mood** (120–160 w) — what the image evokes, kept anchored to concrete detail.
3. **Series / project context** (60–90 w) — how this piece sits in the larger body.
4. **Quiet close** (30–50 w) — ends without summing up. Avoid Fazit-style wraps.

## Sentence rhythm
Deliberate mix. A long, slow sentence followed by something short and definite. Never more than two long sentences in a row. Periods do the work; semicolons build; em-dash reserved for genuine breaks (max 1 per article, ideally 0).

## Vocabulary
- **DE:** alltägliche Sprache mit präzisen Begriffen, wenn nötig. Anglizismen erlaubt, wenn sie das genauere Wort sind — nicht als Pose.
- **EN:** plain, exact diction. Art-historical terms only when they earn their place.
- **ZH:** literarisch zugängliches 书面语, nicht hochgestochen-akademisch. Kalibrierung am ersten echten Output.

## Hard avoids (alle Sprachen)
- Hedging verbs as decoration ("wirkt", "scheint", "seems", "appears")
- Precious adverbs as filler ("etwas", "ein wenig", "fast", "somewhat")
- Interior projection beyond what's visible („Es ist die Stille, in der eine Entscheidung …" without the image showing it)
- Selbstbeweihräuchernd / self-congratulatory talk about work or maker
- „in einer Welt, in der …" / „in a world where …" / „在一个 …… 的世界里"
- atemberaubend / stunning / breathtaking / 令人窒息
- Pathos-Vokabeln: episch, monumental, schicksalhaft, profound, soul-stirring, transcendent
- AI-marketing-Phrasen ("harnessing", "AI-generated masterpiece", "next-generation")
- Em-dash overuse, exclamation marks, hashtags-in-prose
- Rhetorical questions as transitions (1 per article max, only if it carries weight)

## Titles
- 1–5 words, occasionally up to 7. Rotate modes:
  - **poetic-evocative** — *Stilles Licht* / *The Colour That Stays* / *未说出的颜色*
  - **single concept** — *Vorhang* / *Threshold* / *门槛*
  - **question** (only if it carries weight) — *Wer schaut zurück?* / *Whose Hands?* / *谁在看?*
- Avoid: clickbait, ALL CAPS, colons cramming two ideas together.

## Multilingual handling
Three sibling posts per piece (DE / EN / ZH), generated in one pass so voice stays aligned. Each rendered **natively**, not word-for-word — same structure, same mood, same key images, idiomatic in its own language. WordPress wiring (Polylang vs. WPML vs. category-based) → resolved in Phase 2.

## SEO
~330 words is short for ranking competitive terms. Compensate with: per-image ALT-text (one sentence, in locale), meta description ≤ 155 chars in voice, clean slug, 3–6 tags.

## Calibration samples — same imagined image (warm interior, hand resting on a book by an open window)

**DE — *Stilles Licht***

> Das Fenster steht offen. Das Licht fällt schräg herein, warm. Eine Hand liegt auf dem Buch, die andere im Schoß. Mehr zeigt das Bild nicht, und mehr braucht es nicht.
>
> Die Pause hält sich. Sie wird nicht leer, weil das Buch noch offen ist und das Licht sich verschiebt. Gedämpftes Ocker, ein gebrochenes Grün. Die Farben halten den Raum zusammen, ohne ihn zu schließen.

**EN — *Quiet Light***

> The window stands open. Light falls in at an angle, warm. One hand rests on the book, the other in the lap. The painting shows nothing more, and nothing more is needed.
>
> The pause holds. It does not go empty, because the book is still open and the light keeps shifting. Muted ochre, a broken green. The colours hold the room together without closing it.
